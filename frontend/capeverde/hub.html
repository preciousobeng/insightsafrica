Cape Verde Country Profile

All five data products unified — satellite intelligence and socioeconomic indicators combined into a single country risk briefing for Africa's model island democracy.

Product 01
FloodWatch

CHIRPS monthly rainfall across all 9 inhabited islands. 24 months of data (2024–2025). Despite low annual averages (~250mm in Praia), intense convective events send flash floods through narrow ribeiras with little warning — particularly on Santiago, Fogo, and Santo Anão.

Live · 24 months →
🪨
Product 02
QuarryWatch

Sentinel-2 change detection across Cape Verde's volcanic extraction zones — basalt quarrying on Santiago and São Vicente, pozzolana volcanic soil on Santo Anão, and salt flat monitoring on Sal island. NDVI and NDWI change maps track surface disturbance and coastal alteration.

Live · 4 islands →
🌿
Product 03
CropWatch

MODIS 250m NDVI composites across Santiago's Serra Malagueta highlands, Santo Anão's terraced ribeira agriculture (maize, coffee, sugar cane), and São Nicolãu's farmed plateaus. Drought stress scoring through the short rainy season (August–October).

Live · 46 composites →
🌡️
Product 04
HeatWatch

Landsat 9 land surface temperature for Praia (capital, fast-growing coastal city on Santiago), Mindelo (cultural capital on São Vicente, Atlantic port), and Santa Maria (flat arid tourism hub on Sal). Urban heat island intensity measured against Atlantic-exposed rural fringe.

Data gap · no L2SP thermal →

Human Development
HumanWatch

Key socioeconomic indicators — child mortality, poverty, health expenditure, literacy, and life expectancy — sourced from the World Bank Open Data API. Cape Verde consistently ranks among West Africa's highest HDI scores and strongest governance indicators.
