About

What this is

Satellites have been observing West Africa for decades. The data is free, global, and remarkably detailed — CHIRPS rainfall going back to 1981, Sentinel-2 imagery updated every five days, Landsat thermal bands measuring city temperatures since the 1970s. Most of it sits in archives, useful only to those with the tools and time to process it.

InsightsAfrica turns that archive into something readable. Not raw rasters, not academic papers — working dashboards that show whether last month’s rainfall in the Volta Basin was higher than the year before, whether heat is intensifying in Kumasi’s urban core, or whether vegetation around a mining corridor has quietly shifted.

It started with Ghana because the use case was clearest there. West Africa is next. The data is already there — it just needed someone to look at it properly.
