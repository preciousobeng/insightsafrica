Geography & Climate

Cape Verde is a semi-arid volcanic archipelago 500km west of Senegal. 10 islands (9 inhabited) across three clusters: Windward (Barlavento) and Leeward (Sotavento) groups. Narrow volcanic gorges called ribeiras are prone to flash flooding during the Aug–Oct rainy season. Climate: tropical savanna; average rainfall 250mm/year in Praia.

Economic Drivers

Tourism: Sal, Boa Vista (beaches, diving). Fishing: Rich Atlantic stocks; EU agreements. Remittances: Large diaspora (Portugal, Africa, USA). Quarrying: Basalt, pozzolana, salt extraction. Renewable energy: Wind & solar projects emerging.
